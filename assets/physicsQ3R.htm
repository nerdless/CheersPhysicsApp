About two and a half pints!

Carbon dioxide gas is found in beers naturally as a result of the fermentation process. But in some cases additional pressurised carbon dioxide gas is added to your beer to give your beer the white and fluffy head.

To get two and half pints of CO2 into your beer, beer is kept pressurised and the gas dissolves into the liquid. Once the pressure is released, the carbon dioxide comes out as bubbles.

Drop a peanut into your pint. Notice how bubbles start to form around the peanut. These are nucleation points, a rough surface where the dissolved carbon dioxide can form into bubbles. This also works with raisins, ice cubes, slices of lemon, or anything with a rough surface.
