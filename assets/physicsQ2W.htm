It depends on the beer!

The type of glass you have affects the aroma and taste of the beer, how much your hand warms it up and whether your pint can maintain its foamy head.

A beer’s flavours and aromas change with temperature, so a thick glass, or one with a handle, insulates your pint from the heat of your hand.

Some beers like being held closely as the heat from your hand releases flavours. Here a tulip or chalice glass is best as they lend themselves to being cupped.

Flavours and aromas come to your nose by the bubbles in a beer’s head spraying a tiny amount of liquid into the air. Tapered glasses concentrate the aroma and force the drinker’s nose closer to the beer.

Some fizzier beers suit tall thin glasses. Less liquid is in contact with the bottom, preventing too many bubbles from forming in one go and providing plenty of room for a large head.

When you order your next pint ask for an additional half-pint glass. Pour half your pint into the half-pint glass. The beer should taste different depending on which glass you drink from.
